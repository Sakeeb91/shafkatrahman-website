# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Technically curious readers, AI builders, founders, and investors who arrive from social posts, search, or referrals to understand model capabilities, agent systems, infrastructure economics, and applied AI. They need rigorous analysis that remains readable outside a specialist research setting.

## Product Purpose

ShafkatRahman.com is Shafkat Rahman's public home for technical writing, research, advisory work, and selected resources. It should turn short-lived social reach into durable authority, subscriber relationships, and qualified advisory conversations while giving each essay a stable, searchable home.

## Brand Personality

Analytical, independent, and precise. The voice should feel technically fluent without becoming academic theatre, confident without hype, and curious without vagueness.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI landing pages with purple glows, glass cards, or inflated claims.
- Trend-chasing editorial layouts that make the typography louder than the argument.
- Dense research portals that bury the thesis under navigation or metadata.
- Personal sites that read like unfinished resumes instead of active bodies of work.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the argument. A visitor should understand what an article is claiming before encountering supporting detail.
2. Make technical depth readable. Long-form pages need strong hierarchy, measured line length, legible equations, and calm spacing.
3. Show the evidence. Distinguish vendor claims, illustrative calculations, and independently verified results.
4. Preserve continuity. Navigation, theme behavior, metadata, and subscription paths should work consistently from every entry point.
5. Keep the author visible through judgment, not decoration. The site's personality comes from clear analysis and specific choices.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic headings, descriptive alternative text, sufficient contrast in both themes, responsive reading layouts, and reduced-motion behavior for nonessential animation.
